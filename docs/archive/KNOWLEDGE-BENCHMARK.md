# KNOWLEDGE BENCHMARK FRAMEWORK
**Version 1.0 | 9 March 2026**

## The Core Problem

The previous system claimed an agent had "68% knowledge of Marketing" because it read 8 text chunks. That's meaningless.

**Real expertise requires depth across 7 layers:**

1. **Platform Access & Live Data** (0-15%)
2. **Historical Context & Patterns** (15-30%)
3. **Domain-Specific Intelligence** (30-45%)
4. **Technical Infrastructure** (45-55%)
5. **Competitive & Strategic Context** (55-70%)
6. **Team, Process & Execution Capability** (70-85%)
7. **Predictive & Prescriptive Intelligence** (85-100%)

This framework applies to EVERY business function: Finance, Sales, Marketing (all sub-domains), Operations, People, Product, Legal, Strategy.

---

## Example: SEO (One Sub-Domain of Marketing)

### Layer 1: Platform Access (0-15%)

**Connected to:**
- Google Search Console (impressions, clicks, CTR, position per query)
- Google Analytics 4 (organic traffic, conversions, user journeys)
- Ahrefs/Semrush/Moz (backlinks, domain rating, keyword rankings, competitor analysis)
- Google Business Profile (local rankings, reviews)
- CMS (WordPress/Shopify/Webflow - meta tags, schema, page speed)
- Core Web Vitals / PageSpeed Insights (LCP, FID, CLS)

**Without these connections: 0% knowledge.**

Reading a blog post about SEO ≠ expertise.

### Layer 2: Historical Context (15-30%)

**12+ months of data:**
- Ranking history (seasonal patterns, algorithm update impacts)
- Content publication timeline (what performed, what flopped)
- Backlink acquisition history
- Technical change log (site migrations, redesigns, URL restructures)
- Previous SEO work (what agencies tried, what strategies failed)
- Competitor historical performance
- Penalty history (manual actions, algorithmic penalties, recovery)

**Without history: snapshot with no trajectory.**

### Layer 3: Domain Intelligence (30-45%)

**For SEO, this means:**
- Complete keyword universe (every keyword they rank for, want to rank for, should rank for)
- Content inventory with performance (target keyword, ranking, traffic, conversions per page)
- Content gap analysis (topics competitors rank for that they don't)
- Keyword cannibalisation map (multiple pages competing for same keyword)
- Topical authority assessment (how deeply the site covers core topics)
- Search intent mapping (does content match what users are looking for?)
- Conversion path from organic (user searches X, lands on Y, converts via Z)

**Without this: can't make strategic recommendations.**

### Layer 4: Technical Infrastructure (45-55%)

**Must understand:**
- Crawlability (robots.txt, canonical tags, XML sitemaps)
- Site architecture (URL structure, internal linking, orphan pages)
- Page speed (Core Web Vitals per template, render-blocking resources)
- Mobile experience (mobile-first indexing status, usability errors)
- Schema / structured data (Product, FAQ, HowTo markup)
- JavaScript rendering (can Googlebot see the content?)
- HTTPS / security (certificate issues, redirect chains)
- Internationalisation (hreflang, geo-targeting)
- Log file analysis (what Googlebot actually does vs what tools simulate)

**Without this: recommendations might be actively harmful.**

### Layer 5: Competitive & Strategic Context (55-70%)

**Must know:**
- Top 5-10 organic competitors (domain ratings, content strategies, growth trajectories)
- SERP feature analysis (featured snippets, People Also Ask, video carousels)
- Market dynamics (search volumes trending up or down? new competitors?)
- Business model alignment (which keywords actually drive revenue?)
- Resource constraints (content writers available? link building budget? dev capacity?)
- Integration with other channels (SEO vs paid vs social vs email)
- AI Overview / LLM visibility (appearing in Google AI Overviews? ChatGPT citations?)
- Algorithm risk (vulnerability to specific updates)

**Without this: technically correct but strategically useless.**

### Layer 6: Team & Execution (70-85%)

**Must understand:**
- Who does SEO? (dedicated person? generalist? freelancer? agency? skill level?)
- Content production capability (who writes? how fast? what quality?)
- Developer access (can technical changes be implemented? how long is dev queue?)
- Budget allocation (spent on tools, content, links, agencies)
- Approval processes (does content need legal review? brand approval? how long?)
- Reporting cadence (who reports on SEO? how often? to whom?)
- Historical team knowledge (institutional memory of what was tried and failed)

**Without this: can't assess if recommendations are executable.**

### Layer 7: Predictive & Prescriptive (85-100%)

**Agent can:**
- Forecast traffic (based on ranking trajectories, seasonal patterns, planned content)
- Model impact ("publish 10 articles targeting X cluster → expect Z traffic in W weeks")
- Prioritise actions (given business goals, resource constraints, current performance → here's what to do next and why)
- Detect anomalies ("traffic dropped 15% Tuesday - canonical tag accidentally removed during deploy - fix this")
- Competitive alerting ("Competitor X published 12 articles in your core cluster, increased DR 5 points in 30 days")
- Attribution ROI ("blog post from 4 months ago generated 340 visits, 23 leads, 4 customers worth £18.2K LTV")
- Cross-channel optimisation ("top organic page already owns position 1 - running branded PPC wastes £800/month")

**This is the pinnacle. Most "AI SEO agents" on the market operate at Layer 1-2 only.**

## Scaling Across All Business Functions

The same 7-layer depth applies to every domain.

### Finance & Accounting (Summary)

**Layer 1:** Xero/QuickBooks connected, bank feeds, Stripe, payroll  
**Layer 2:** 3+ years P&L, balance sheet trends, seasonal cash flow patterns  
**Layer 3:** Unit economics, CAC, LTV, payment terms, collection rates  
**Layer 4:** Technical health (reconciliation cadence, tax compliance, audit readiness)  
**Layer 5:** Funding runway, debt covenants, tax planning, R&D credits, exit valuation

### Sales & Revenue (Summary)

**Layer 1:** CRM (HubSpot/Pipedrive/Salesforce), email sequences, call recordings  
**Layer 2:** Win/loss analysis 12+ months, seasonal patterns, deal velocity, churn data  
**Layer 3:** Pipeline intelligence (every deal: stage, value, probability, owner, next action)  
**Layer 4:** Sales process steps, qualification criteria, handoff from marketing  
**Layer 5:** Competitive intelligence (who are they losing to? why? objection patterns)  
**Layer 6:** Sales team structure, quota attainment, ramp time for new hires

**Paid Search:** Google Ads connected, CPA/ROAS/quality scores, impression share, search term analysis

**Organic Social:** Native analytics, engagement rate, reach, follower growth, content performance  
**Content:** CMS, editorial calendar, traffic per post, conversion from content  
**PR:** Media monitoring, backlink tracking, share of voice, brand mentions  
**CRO:** Hotjar, VWO, conversion rate by page, experiment results, UX friction points

### Operations & Delivery (Summary)

**Layer 1:** Project management (Asana/Monday), support desk (Zendesk/Intercom), fulfilment systems

**Layer 1:** GitHub/GitLab, CI/CD, error tracking (Sentry), feature flags, analytics (Mixpanel/Amplitude)  
**Layer 2:** Historical feature usage, NPS by feature, previous product decisions  
**Layer 3:** Roadmap with prioritisation rationale, user feedback patterns

**Layer 5:** Architecture (tech stack, integrations, data flows, scaling constraints)  
**Layer 6:** Development process (sprint velocity, cycle time, bug rate, testing coverage)

### Legal & Compliance (Summary)

**Layer 1:** All contracts (customer, supplier, employee, partner), T&Cs, privacy policy  
**Layer 2:** Historical legal activity (disputes, resolutions, policy changes)  
**Layer 3:** Regulatory landscape (industry regs, upcoming changes, compliance obligations)

## The Benchmark in Practice

**For OYNB (test company):**

Current system says: **68% Intelligence Score, 60% Marketing knowledge**

Reality using this framework: **<10% total knowledge, ~3% Marketing knowledge**

**Why?**
- 8 text chunks about "marketing" ≠ knowing the SEO situation
- Not connected to Google Search Console, GA4, Ahrefs, or any marketing tools
- No historical data, no competitive intelligence, no team context
- Can't answer basic questions: "What's your domain authority?" "Who writes content?" "What are your top 3 ranking keywords?"

**What 50% Marketing knowledge would actually require:**
- Google Search Console + GA4 + Ahrefs connected
- 12+ months of ranking history
- Content inventory with performance data
- Competitive landscape mapped
- Team structure understood (who does SEO? content production capability?)
- Budget allocation known
- Can answer: "Here are your top 10 opportunities, rank-ordered by impact and effort"

## Why This Matters

**The Nathan Problem:**
Agencies gather 10% of needed information → make assumptions → break dependencies they didn't know existed → kill the business.

**The Founder Engine Solution:**
Gather 50%+ knowledge per domain BEFORE making recommendations. Ask clarifying questions for gaps. Show founder exactly what the agent knows and doesn't know. Build trust through honesty, not fake percentages.

**The Pricing Justification:**
At £1K-£5K/month, this is priced against fractional CFOs (£3-5K/month), management consultants (£5-10K/month), and advisory retainers (£2-5K/month).
